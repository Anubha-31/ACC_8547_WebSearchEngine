A: Going by the trend of past five years, CAT 2021 exam date should be November 28 this year. However, the exam date will be officially announced by IIM in the last week of July. Q: Which IIM will conduct CAT 2021? A: CAT exam is conducted annually. Every year, one of the IIMs is given the responsibility to conduct the exam. A CAT convener is appointed for this purpose. CAT 2021 will be conducted by . Prof M P Ram Mohan, Faculty in Business Policy, IIM Ahmedabad has been appointed as the CAT 2021 exam convener. The fee amount is to be paid in online mode only via debit/credit card or net banking. CAT exam application fee is Rs 2,000 Q: Is there an age limit to appear in CAT exam? A: No, there is no age limit defined to appear in the CAT exam. Any candidate who is a graduate or is in the final year of graduation is eligible to apply for CAT. Working professionals are also eligible to appear in CAT. Q: What are the scholarship schemes in IIMs that candidates appearing in CAT can avail of upon admission? A: IIMs provide scholarships to meritorious candidates and the ones belonging to the economically weaker section. The candidates can avail this benefit by submitting the relevatn documents at the time of admission. Also Read: IIM Fees & Scholarships Q. I am in my second year of graduation. Can I appear for CAT for practice purpose? A: No, the candidate must fulfill the minimum eligibility criteria for CAT to be able to appear for the exam. If you haven't completed graduation and/or are not in the final year of bachelor's degree, your application form will be rejected. Q: When will CAT 2021 result be announced? A: CAT 2021 result will be announced in the first week of January 2022. The scorecards are available for download from official website of CAT. Post the declaration of results, IIM shortlisting will begin for MBA admission 2021-23 batch. Q: Can I appear for more than one slot for CAT exam? A: No, each candidate can appear for CAT exam in one slot only. The test slot is allotted to the candidate by the exam authority. To ensure every candidate attempts the exam in only one slot, admit cards, photo IDs, candidate photo, and biometric record will be verified. Q: Are there any special provisions for PwD candidates? A: Yes, there are special provisions for PwD candidates, such as scribe assistance and extra exam hours. To avail these facilities, the candidates have to upload PwD and scribe certificates along with the CAT application form and carry the same to the exam centre. Q: Is the extra one hour for PwD candidates distributed among three sections of CAT question paper? A: Yes. The PwD candidates get 20 minutes extra per CAT exam section. This extra hour is divided equally among the three sections. The candidates need to fill the PwD form at the time of application submission and carry related documents to the exam centre to avail time and other relaxation. Q: I belong to EWS category, which documents do I need to upload with CAT form? A: You are required to upload an EWS category form available on the CAT website along with the CAT application form. The form should be duly filled and self-attested before submission. Q: What is the marking scheme for CAT? Is there negative marking? A: In CAT exam, three marks are awarded for every right attempt and minus one mark is deducted for every wrong attempt. There is no negative marking for unattempted questions so the candidates must choose their option wisely. Q: Can I select my preference for shift and test date? A: The CAT exam is conducted in a single day in multiple shifts - two to three. The exam slot is allotted by the exam authority as per availability of seats at a particular exam centre.
