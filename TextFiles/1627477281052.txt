The two-year MBA programme is a gateway to a plethora of job and entrepreneurial opportunities. This course provides knowledge and training in business administration and operations which are essential for running an organisation. The MBA programme develops communication skills and leadership qualities in the individual, as well.
Students from any background, including Science, Commerce and Humanities can pursue MBA. The is the most popular among all, and includes theory classes, practical projects, and internship. Fresh graduates as well as candidates with a few years of work experience opt for a full-time MBA.

MBA Colleges in India Eligibility Criteria
The eligibility criteria for MBA course is graduation or equivalent from a recognised university, however the minimum score requirement may vary for niche specialisations.

Candidates must have passed graduation in any discipline or equivalent from a recognised university as a basic eligibility criteria for MBA (full-time)
Most of the institutes follow the minimum score criteria in graduation as per the AICTE i.e. 50 per cent aggregate or equivalent and five per cent relaxation to students belonging to reserved categories.
Professional degree holders such as MBBS/CA/CS/ICWAI, etc. are also eligible to pursue MBA
Top MBA Colleges in India with NIRF Ranking and Shiksha Rating (Aggregate)
The Ministry of Education, Government of India, releases annual ranking of top MBA colleges in India under the National Institutional Ranking Framework or NIRF. The all India ranking is given to 100 best MBA colleges in India on the basis of several parameters such as teaching, learning and resources, research and professional practices, graduation outcomes, outreach and inclusivity and perception.

FAQs on Top MBA Colleges in India

Q: Which is the top MBA college in India?
A: The top MBA college in India as per the latest NIRF rankings is IIM Bangalore. It is followed by IIM Ahmedabad on number 2, IIM Calcutta on number 3, IIM Lucknow on number 4 and IIM Udaipur on number 5.

Q: Which is MBA college is better? Private or government?
A: Any MBA college which is UGC recognised and AICTE is approved is good, because they offer the latest curriculum. However, between private and government college, it is upto the candidate to decided which MBA college to choose. While private institutions have better infrastructure, the government institutions charge low fees.

Q: How can I decide which is the best MBA college for me?
A: The candidates must keep in mind the following while choosing an MBA college – government recognition, degree/diploma, curriculum and pedagogy, infrastructure, ranking, location, fees and placement record.

Q: Which are the top ranked MBA colleges apart from IIMs?
A: Apart from the IIMs, the top ranked MBA institutes in India according to NIRF include, department of management studies of the IITs, MDI Gurgaon, SPJIMR Mumbai, IMI Delhi, IMI Kolkata, IIFM Bhopal, XLRI Jamshedpur, Thiagarajar School of Management, Jaipuria Institute of Management, Fore School of Management, etc.

Q: Which Indian MBA colleges are ranked worldwide?
A: IIM Bangalore, IIM Ahmedabad, IIM Calcutta, ISB Hyderabad are ranked among best management colleges in worldwide rankings. In the top 100 schools in Financial Times’ Global MBA Ranking 2021, ISB has been ranked 23 in the world, IIM Bangalore 35, IIM Calcutta and IIM Ahmedabad 44 and 48, and IIM Indore 94.

Q: What are the different specialisations offered by MBA Colleges in India?
A: Top specialisations offered at top MBA Colleges in India are:

General Management
Marketing
Sales
Human Resources
Operations Management
Healthcare
Business Analytics
IT & Systems
Finance Management
Industrial Engineering

Q: Which are the best MBA colleges in India based on placements?
A: Top five MBA Colleges in India based on placements trends are:

IIM Bangalore
IIM Calcutta
IIM Ahmedabad
IIM Lucknow
IIT Kharagpur-DoMS

Q: What are the job profiles offered after completing MBA in India?
A: Some of the job profiles offered after completing MBA are:

Finance Consultant
Finance Manager
Marketing Manager
Sales Manager
Human Resources Manager
Operations Manager
Product Manager
Data Analytics Manager
Digital Marketing Manager
Advertising Sales Manager
NGO Manager

Q: What is the estimated fee for studying MBA in India?
Q: It is estimated that pursuing MBA in India from top-rated colleges is moderately-priced. Nearly 63% of the colleges in India have annual fee less than Rs 10 lakh. Some of these colleges are:

DoMS IIT Kanpur
DoMS IIT Delhi
DoMS IIT Roorkee
DoMS IIT Madras
SJMSOM IIT Bombay
DoMS IIT Kharagpur

Q: Is there any entrance exam for MBA in India?
A: There are various entrance exams accepted by top MBA colleges in India such as CAT, XAT, GMAT, CMAT, MAT, NMAT, ATMA, IBSAT, MAH CET, SNAP, etc.

Q: What are my study options after completing an MBA course in India?
A: After completing MBA, the aspirants who want to go for higher studies or certification can pursue the following courses:

Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA)
Financial Risk Manager (FRM) Exam
Certified Information Systems Auditor (CISA)
Certification in Risk Management Assurance (CRMA)
Certified in Production and Inventory Management (CPIM)
Project Management Professional (PMP)
