What is SNAP Exam?
Symbiosis National Aptitude Test or is a management aptitude test conducted by the Symbiosis International (Deemed) University for admission to the MBA/PGDM programmes offered by its 16 constituent institutes. The SNAP exam paper comprises questions from , and and the mode of exam is online. After clearing the SNAP exam, candidates have to participate in Group Exercise (GE), Personal Interaction (PI) and Written Ability Test (WAT), conducted by SIU institutes individually in February and March. In 2020, the The same pattern is expected to be followed for SNAP 2021 exam.

SNAP Eligibility Criteria
Given below is the eligibility criteria for SNAP exam 2021.



Graduates in any discipline with at least 50 per cent aggregate (45 per cent for SC/ST/PWD candidates) or equivalent from recognised university or institute can apply for SNAP 2021



Final year students are also eligible to apply for SNAP Test, but their admission will remain provisional until submission of the passing certificate

Important FAQs Regarding SNAP


Q: What is the application fee of SNAP Test?
A: The SNAP application fee is Rs 1,750 to be paid in online mode only through credit/debit card or internet banking. The application fee is to be paid for successful submission of the form. The fee amount is non-refundable.

Q: What is the process to get admission through SNAP 2021?
A: The admission process includes SNAP Test, Group Exercise (GE), Personal Interaction (PI), and Writing Aptitude Test (WAT). Final admission is granted to candidates on the basis of SNAP scores and performance in GE, PI and WAT.

Q: How many times can I appear in SNAP 2021?
A: As per the latest exam pattern, SNAP 2021 will be conducted thrice between December and January. The candidates will be given two attempts. The score of best of the two SNAP attempts will be considered for shortlisting for SIU admission.

Q: Is there any reservation policy in SNAP?
A: There is no reservation policy in SNAP but there is one for admission to SIU. Given below is the sanctioned intake under the govt approved reservation policy

Q: What is the syllabus of SNAP 2021?
A: SNAP syllabus includes all the important topics of General English, Analytical & Logical Reasoning and Quantitative, Data Interpretation & Data Sufficiency.

Q: Will SNAP 2021 be delayed due to COVID-19?
A: It is unlikely that SNAP 2021 exam will be delayed due to COVID-19 as it is administered in December and Januarry. It is expected that by that time, COVID-19 pandemic will be very much in control.

Q: Will there be impact of COVID-19 on SNAP 2021?
A: It is unlikely that there will be any impact on COVID-19 this year too. With the vaccination programme in full swing and daily cases decreasing, it is expected thay by December when the SNAP exam is conducted, everything will be back to normal.

Q: When will SNAP 2021 registrations begin?
A: SNAP 2021 registration process is expected to begin by the third week of August and continue till last week of November. The application forms will b available on the official website of SNAP 2021 in this duration.

Q: Is there any age limit to appear for SNAP?
A: No, there is no age limit to appear for SNAP. However, the candidate must have completed Graduation or most be in the final year of their Bachelor's degree. Working professionals are also eligible to appear for the test.

Q: What is the marks relaxation scheme for SC/ST candidates?
A: For SC/ST candidates, the minimum marks required is 45 per cent aggregate in graduation (any stream). For General category candidates, the minimum marks requirement is 50 per cent in aggregate.

Q: Is the eligibility criteria same for all MBA courses offered by SIU?
A: No, the eligibility criteria for all MBA courses is not common. For example, the eligibility criteria for MBA (Infrastructure Development and Management) is BE/BTech, BArch or BPlan. The candidates must check the course-wise eligibility criteria for the particular course before filling the SNAP application form.

Q: Is there any cut off for admission through SNAP?
A: Yes, each institute releases marks for admission, on the basis of which candidates are screened for GE/PI and WAT. The candidates have to fill an online form released after the release of SNAP scorecard.
