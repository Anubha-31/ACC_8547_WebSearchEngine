West Bengal Joint Entrance Examination (WBJEE) is a state-level exam conducted by WBJEEB. The exam is a gateway for candidates seeking admission into and courses at universities, government colleges and self-financing engineering & technical institutions in West Bengal. Candidates are required to comply with WBJEE 2021 eligibility criteria to successfully apply for the Joint Entrance Exam. Candidates failing to comply with the eligibility norms will not be allowed to appear for the exam. WBJEE eligibility criteria might vary for different courses and at the institute level. Take a look at the WBJEE 2021 eligibility criteria below. Eligibility criteria for appearing in WBJEE 2021 Nationality The aspirant must be an Indian citizen Age restriction A candidate must be at least 17 years of age (as on December 31, 2021) to appear for the exam or or should be born on or before December 31, 2004. This means an aspirant should have been born on or before December 31, 2004. There is no upper age limit for appearing in the WBJEE 2021. However, for admission to the Marine Engineering Course, the lower age limit is 17 years as on December 31, 2021, and the upper age limit is 25 years as on December 31, 2021. WBJEE 2021 Eligibility criteria for B.Tech admissions Aspirants should have passed Class 12 or equivalent with Physics and Mathematics along with any one of listed below subjects as compulsory subjects with individual pass marks (in both theory and practical wherever applicable) in all the three subjects in regular class mode. Chemistry Biotechnology Biology Computer Science Computer Application Technical Vocational Subject Aspirants must have secured a minimum 45 per cent marks (40 per cent in case of candidates belonging to reserved category such as SC, ST, OBC-A, OBC-B, PwD) in the above three subjects taken together. Candidates should have qualified English in Class 12 exam with at least 30 per cent marks. The Board of the Qualifying Examination must be recognised by the Central Government or State Government concerned. Check the detailed WBJEE 2021 Eligibility Criteria here! WBJEEB will start WBJEE 2021 counselling after the exam is conducted. Q. Is WBJEE conducted in online or offline mode? A. WBJEE will be conducted offline in pen and paper-based test mode. Q. What are the scholarship schemes offered in West Bengal through WBJEE? A. WBJEE aspirants are offered scholarships under the Tuition Fee Waiver (TFW) schemes of WBJEEB. The scholarship is applicable to those students who have performed well in academics and WBJEE exam. These schemes are applicable for candidates seeking admission to the UG courses in Engineering/Architecture/Pharmacy. The amount of scholarship lies between Rs 1,400 to 1,500 per month. Q. Why is WBJEE written exam important? A. Admissions to the B.Tech and B.Pharma courses offered at colleges in West Bengal are done through WBJEE exam. This makes it mandatory for candidates to appear for the exam and then participate in the e-counselling for seat allotment. Q. Why is it important to verify WBJEE eligibility criteria before applying for the exam? A. It is important for candidates to comply with the WBJEE eligibility norms. Candidates, who do not meet the minimum eligibility requirements, will not be allowed to appear for the exam and their applications will be cancelled by WBJEEB. Q. What is GMR? A. GMR is the General Merit List released by WBJEEB for admission to all the Degree Level Engineering / Technology / Architecture Courses available in all enlisted Engineering / Technology Colleges and Universities within the state of West Bengal.
