What is IBSAT?
IBS Aptitude Test or IBSAT is a national level entrance exam conducted by (a deemed to be University) for admission to MBA/PGPM programmes. As per the IBSAT 2021 notification, the registration for IBSAT 2021 has commenced from July 6. Earlier, IBSAT 2021 registration was scheduled to commence from July 1. , namelyIBS Hyderabad, IBS Mumbai, IBS Bengaluru, IBS Pune, IBS Gurgaon, IBS Kolkata, IBS Ahmedabad, IBS Jaipur and IBS Dehradun. IBSAT is a two-hour Computer-based test (CBT) comprising multiple-choice questions. IBSAT is a computer-based test that is generally conducted over a window of two days in December. Last year, the exam was conducted in an online proctored mode or an Internet-based Test (IBT) mode. This step was taken as a response measure to the coronavirus pandemic, to avoid public gathering. This year also, it is expected that IBSAT will be conducted in IBT mode.

IBSAT 2021 Admission Process
The admission process for IBSAT includes five stages — filling application form, issuing of admit card, IBSAT 2021 exam, declaration of result and selection process for shortlisted candidates. Each stage in the IBSAT Admission process is explained below:




IBSAT Admission Process

Step 1: IBSAT 2021 application form
The first step of the admission process begins with filling the . Candidates need to fill the application form within the slated time, as there would not be any provision for submitting the IBSAT 2021 application forms beyond the deadline. IBS aspirants can apply for the IBSAT 2021 exam online. The application process will start with the creation of login ID and password and will end with paying the application fee of Rs 1,800. IBSAT is conducted in multiple test cities across the country.
IBSAT 2021 application form

Step 2: IBSAT 2021 admit card
IBSAT 2021 admit card will be issued only to the registered candidates. Without the hall ticket, no applicant will be allowed to enter the exam centre. will be available online on the official website. To download the hall ticket, key in the registration number, date of birth and password.
IBSAT 2021 admit card

Step 3: IBSAT 2021 exam
IBSAT exam is conducted in the Computer-based Test (CBT) mode. Last year, the exam was conducted in online proctored mode, wherein candidates could take the test from their home. This year, in case the exam is conducted in test centres like the previous years, then it is likely that IBSAT 2021 will be held in 95 cities across India. The test duration is two hours and the total number of multiple-choice questions (MCQs) is 140.

FAQs Related to IBSAT 2021

Q: How to apply for IBSAT 2021?
IBSAT application forms will be available in online mode on the official website of IBS India. Candidates can apply for the exam by registering on the official website.
A:

Q: What is the fee structure for the MBA programme at IBS campuses?
The course fee is Rs 8.03 lakh at IBS Ahmedabad, IBS Bengaluru, IBS Gurgaon, IBS Kolkata, IBS Mumbai and IBS Pune while it is Rs 5.69 lakhs at IBS Dehradun and IBS Jaipur campuses.
A:

Q: What is the IBS selection briefing?
The selection briefings are a series of seminars to provide candidates with an insight into the programmes and processes at IBS. Selection briefings are followed by Group Discussion and Personal Interview.
A:

Q: What is the exam pattern of IBSAT?
The paper is divided into four sections - Verbal Ability, Reading Comprehension, Quantitative Ability and Data Adequacy & Data Interpretation. The total number of questions will be 140 and the duration of the test is two hours.
A:

Q: Which books to study for the IBSAT exam?
Some best books for IBSAT exam include Mathematics for MBA entrance exam by RS Aggarwal, Quantitative aptitude for MBA Entrance Examination by Abhijit Guha, Objective English For Competitive Examination by Thorpe, Data Interpretation MBA Entrance by Jaiswal, Verbal Ability Guide for CAT by PT Education Ltd.
A:

Q: What is the difficulty level of IBSAT exam?
Amongst all the national exams that are conducted for MBA admissions, IBSAT exam is considered to be of moderate difficulty level.
A:

Q How can I prepare for the IBSAT exams?
The best way to prepare for the IBSAT exam is to first know the exam pattern, structure and syllabus. After that, aspirants should collect good resources like preparation books and study guides. The second is to make a timeline to complete the syllabus and take up mock tests to understand individual pace.
A:

Q: What is the validity of IBSAT exam score?
The validity of the IBSAT exam is for one year. Applying to MBA programme of any nine ICFAI business schools is based on the score obtained in the entrance test in the same academic year. For example, IBSAT 2021 score will be valid for admission to 2022-23 academic session only.
A:

Q: Is there negative marking in the exam?
IBSAT exam has in total 140 questions (one mark each) and there is no negative marking for the wrong answers. In case of a wrong attempt, zero marks will be awarded.
A:

Q: What is the mode of exam for IBSAT?
IBSAT exam is computer-based. Last year, the exam was conducted in an Internet-Based Test (IBT) mode or a remote proctored mode due to the COVID-19 pandemic. This year, the exam might be conducted in test centres located across 95 cities depending on the discretion of the exam authorities.
A:

Q: How many questions are there in the IBSAT exam?
There will be 140 questions in a total of one mark each. The VA section will have 50 Questions, RC will have 30 Questions, QA will have 30 questions and DA/DI will have 30 questions.
A:

Q: Can students in their graduation apply for IBSAT exam?
Yes, as per the eligibility criteria of the IBSAT exam, any candidate who is in the final year of their bachelor's degree can apply for the IBSAT exam. However, they will have to submit their passing certificate by August.
A:

Q: What is the fee for PGPM program at IBS Ahmedabad, IBS Bengaluru, IBS Gurgaon, IBS Kolkata, IBS Mumbai and IBS Pune?
The fee for PGPM program at IBS Ahmedabad, IBS Bengaluru, IBS Gurgaon, IBS Kolkata, IBS Mumbai and IBS Pune is approx. Rs 4 lakh per annum and is subject to change.
A:

Q: Is work experience necessary criteria for the IBSAT selection process?
No. The candidate's work experience is not counted in the selection process. However, it is a desirable parameter that the selection panel takes into consideration for candidate's selection.
A:

Q: What is the fee for the MBA programme at IBS Dehradun and IBS Jaipur?
The fee for two years MBA programme at IBS Dehradun, and IBS Jaipur is Rs 5.69 lakh. The fee is to be paid in installments semester wise.
A:
