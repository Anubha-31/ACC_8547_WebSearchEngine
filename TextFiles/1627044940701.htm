in India 2021 Pharmacy is a Science that links Medical Sciences with Chemistry. This field of Health Science plays an enormous role in the testing, discovery, production, research and use of medicines and drugs. The Pharmacy courses provide the students with an understanding of different aspects of medicines, drugs, chemical structure, formulation, etc. The rapid growth in the Pharmaceutical industry and research has led to an increase of students interest in the Pharmacy field as a profession. Pharmacy courses are a gateway to the Healthcare and Medical industry. The programmes consist of subjects such as Pharmaceuticals, Pharmacology, Pharmacovigilance, Pharmaceutical Chemistry and Pharmacognosy and are approved by the PCI (Pharmacy Council of India) and AICTE (All India Council of Technical Education), New Delhi. Aspirants who wish to pursue a career in Pharmaceuticals industry must have the following skill-sets: Medicinal and scientific research skills Curiosity and persuasive skills Communication skills and interpersonal skills Adaptation ability Therapeutic and counselling skills Medical writing and ethics Business skills such as marketing and organising Technical skills Sharp memory and good knowledge Determinant and consistency India is home to some of the top-rated colleges/ universities to pursue like Jamia Hamdard University (Delhi), Punjab University - PUCHD (Chandigarh), National Institute of Pharmaceutical Education and Research S.A.S. The top-rated Pharmacy colleges in India offer different types of courses. Over 1,300 colleges offer BPharma course, 750 offer MPharma course, 690 offer UG Diploma and 217 offer Pharm D course. Pharmacy Courses Admission Eligibility Criteria Pharmacy courses can be pursued at Diploma, UG, PG and PhD levels. The eligibility criteria differs as per the course selected by the candidate. However, given below is a broad eligibility criteria for Diploma, UG, PG and PhD Pharmacy courses for the reference of candidates: Candidates must have passed Class 10 with Science subjects i.e. Physics, Chemistry, Biology and Mathematics. Diploma (DPharma): : Candidates must have passed Class 12 with a combination of either Physics, Chemistry and Biology or Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics and secured at least 50% aggregate score from a recognised board. The course is of four years duration. UG level (BPharma) : Candidates must have passed BPharma with at least 50% aggregate score from a pharmacy college as approved by the Pharmacy Council of India (PCI). The course is of two years duration. PG level (MPharma) PhD level: 6-year Pharm D course: Candidates must have completed 10+2 level of education from any state/national education board. They must have studied Physics and Chemistry as compulsory subjects with Mathematics or Biology as additional subjects. They must have completed 17 years of age in the year of admission. Or Candidates holding a diploma in pharmacy are also eligible for Pharm. D course provided they fulfill all other conditions as well. 3-year Pharm D course (Post Baccalaureate) – Candidates passed in Bachelor of Pharmacy course from an institute recognized under Pharmacy Council of India are eligible for Pharm. D course. They must have completed higher secondary education i.e. 10+2 or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics or Biology subjects.
