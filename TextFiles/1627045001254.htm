UPTET 2021 notification Uttar Pradesh Teacher Eligibility Test (UPTET) is a state-level exam conducted once a year to enable candidates to gain eligibility for primary (Classes 1-5) and upper primary (Classes 6-8) level teachers in the schools of Uttar Pradesh. The exam is conducted by the Uttar Pradesh Basic Education Board (UPBEB). UPTET exam is conducted in two shifts for two papers 1 and 2. UPTET Paper 1 is conducted for candidates who plan to become teachers of Classes 1-5. On the other hand, UPTET Paper 2 is for candidates who plan to become teachers of Classes 6-8. Candidates who wish to become teachers of Classes 1-8 need to appear in both papers. Both the UPTET papers are conducted on the same day in offline mode as pen-and-paper based tests. A: Yes, the UPTET 2021 exam has been postponed by the Uttar Pradesh Government in the wake of the COVID-19 pandemic. Q: When will the UPTET 2021 notification release? A: The UPTET notification was set to release on May 11, 2021, however, the release has now been postponed due to the COVID-19. The notification is likely to release soon. Q: What is the age limit to apply for the UPTET exam? A: Candidates should be a minimum of 18 and a maximum of 35 years of age to apply for the UPTET exam. Q: What is the validity of the UPTET eligibility certificate? A: The UPTET eligibility certificate was earlier valid for a period of five years. However, not the UPTET certificate validity has been extended to lifetime. Q: How many papers does one need to give to qualify the UPTET exam? A: Candidates planning to teach classes 1 to 5 need to qualify UPTET paper 1. Candidates planning to teach classes 6 to 8 need to qualify for UPTET paper 2. Candidates planning to teach classes 1 to 8 need to qualify both the papers. Q: How many attempts can a candidate take at the UPTET exam? A: There is no limit on the number of attempts a candidate can take at the UPTET exam. This means that if a candidate meets the eligibility criteria for the exam he or she can take the UPTET exam as many times as they want. Q: Is there any negative marking in the UPTET exam? A: No, there is no negative marking in the UPTET exam. Candidates are awarded 1 mark for each correct answer and there is no deduction of marks for incorrect answers. Q: In which language is the UPTET exam conducted? A: The UPTET exam is conducted in English and Hindi languages. Candidates need to choose the medium of the exam while applying online for the examination. Q: How many times is UPTET conducted in a year? A: The UPTET exam is generally conducted once a year. The UPTET 2021 exam date is not yet announced. Q: How does a candidate get a job after clearing the UPTET exam? A: After a candidate qualifies the UPTET exam, he/she is issued an eligibility certificate. The candidate can apply for different teaching jobs in the schools of Uttar Pradesh on the basis of the UPTET eligibility certificate. It may be noted that the UPTET exam is only an eligibility criterion and does not guarantee a job to the candidate. Q: How is the UPTET exam centre allotted to a candidate? A: The UPTET exam centre is allotted to a candidate on the basis of the city preferences mentioned by him/her in the application form. Q: What is the minimum eligibility criteria to apply for the UPTET exam? A: Candidates should have passed the class 12 exam or completed their graduation in order to be eligible for the UPTET exam.
