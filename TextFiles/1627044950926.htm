Courses UG Courses PG Courses Top Ranked Colleges Top Exams Articles What is Dental? Dental or dentistry is a specialisation or branch of medical sciences, that deals with oral health. To put it simply, Dental courses can be referred to as the study of teeth, gums and other organs in the mouth. Generally, Dental courses are taught at medical colleges at degree level, such as Bachelors or Masters in Dental Surgeries. A number of students or aspirants plan to pursue Dental Diploma or Dental Certification courses. Dental is a vast branch of Medicine that teaches students about the various oral organs, their structures, dental anatomy, diseases and their diagnosis and treatment. Dental syllabus, course curriculum and specialisations vary from course to course and institute to institute. However, the core curriculum or Dental subjects are the same. This page of Shiksha brings to you detailed information about Dental courses, including eligibility, admission process, curriculum, specialisations and career opportunities. Dental Courses Eligibility Criteria The eligibility criteria for the Dental courses vary from programme to programme. Let us take a look at the programme-wise eligibility criteria of Dental courses. Eligibility Criteria for BDS Programme Candidates should pass Class 12 in Science stream with Physics, Chemistry and Biology as core subjects They should obtain minimum 50 per cent aggregate (General category students) and 45 per cent (reserved category candidates) Their age should range between 17 years and 25 years They should qualify for NEET Eligibility Criteria for MDS Programme Candidates should have a degree from a dental college/institute recognised by Dental Council India BDS They must have completed their Internship for BDS before taking admission in the course MDS Candidates should secure minimum 55 per cent aggregate of all subjects of the BDS course They should qualify for MDS entrance exam Eligibility Criteria for Dental Diploma Programmes For undergraduate , candidates should pass Class 12 with minimum 50 per cent aggregate Dental Diploma programmes They must have studied Science stream, with Physics, Chemistry and Biology/Biochemistry as core subjects i n Class 11 and Class 12 P ostgraduate dental diploma should have completed BDS or undergraduate Dental Diploma programme and completed compulsory rotatory internship by the time of admission Eligibility Criteria for Dental Certificate Programmes C andidates should complete a bachelor’s level Dental programme from a recognised institute They should complete the compulsory rotatory internship followed by the course completion Required Skillsets for Dental Courses Candidates aiming to pursue a Dental course should possess the following qualities or skillsets: Attention to details Manual dexterity Communication skills Problem-solving ability Compassion Composure Interpersonal skills Team player Adaptability Quick learning ability Conceptual clarity and subject knowledge understanding Organisational skills Admission Process for Dental Courses Admission to Dental courses is conducted on the basis of entrance exams. A: The duration of the Dental courses vary from programme to programme and also according to the levels. BDS, which is a full-time UG level course is of a four-year duration. MDS or the Master of Dental Surgery, which is a postgraduate programme, is of two to three years duration. The Dental Diploma level programmes are for two years, whereas the duration of Dental Certificate programmes is six months to one year. Q: What are the top courses in Dental? A: The top or the most preferred Dental courses are the full-time BDS and MDS programmes. Q: Which is the best Dental course? A: While each and every Dental courses are designed keeping in mind the students’ levels and requirements, which would enrich their knowledge on Dental medicine based on their current qualification. Hence, it is up to the students to choose the course wisely. Q: Is there any Dental course that I can pursue without taking NEET? A: Aspirants can take admission to Dental Diploma programmes or Dental Certificate programmes without appearing for the NEET UG or NEET MDS exams. Q: Which are the top colleges offering Dental courses in India? A: Some of the top Dental colleges in India are Maulana Azad Institute of Dental Science Delhi, Manipal College of Dental Sciences Udupi, Dr DY Patil Vidyapeeth Pune, Saveetha Institute of Medical and Technical Sciences Chennai, to name a few. Q: What is the average fee of Dental courses? A: The average fee of Dental courses vary from programme to programme and institute to institute. While the tuition fee of BDS ranges from Rs 15,000 to Rs 12,00,000, that of MDS courses is around Rs 2 lakh to Rs 5 lakh. The Dental Diploma programme fees range from Rs 5,000 to Rs 20,000 in Government colleges and Rs 50,000 to Rs 2 lakh in private colleges. Dental Certificate programme fees range from Rs 15,000 to Rs 5 lakh. Q: Is it mandatory to study Biology to for pursuing Dental courses? A: Yes, in order to pursue Dental courses, applicants or students must have studied Biology as a core subject along with Physics and Chemistry in Class 11 and Class 12. Q: Which are some of the top specialisations of Dental programmes? A: Some of the top Dental specialisations include Endodontic, Prosthodontics, Periodontics, Oral and Maxillofacial surgery, to name a few. Q: What is Dental course is a better option for higher studies, MDS or Dental certification? A: After completing BDS programmes, the best option is to pursue an MDS programme. In case they do not have the option to invest two to three years for the MDS programme, they may opt for Dental Certificate programmes. Q: Can Dental students perform surgery? A: Students who have pursued BDS or MDS programmes are qualified to perform oral or dental surgeries. Q: Is it compulsory to undergo an internship during Dental courses? A: Yes, as per the curriculum of the full-time Dental courses, students have to undergo compulsory rotatory internship.
