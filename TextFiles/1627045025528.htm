Download Guide clickHere Popular Colleges Top Courses Top Exams Q&A Articles What is BBA LL.B.? is a five-year course on completion of which the aspirants are offered a professional degree. BBA LLB is an integrated course wherein aspirants are taught subjects related to Business Administration as well as Law. As part of a Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) LLB course aspirants are first taught Commerce subjects such as Principles of Management, Financial Accounting, Computer Applications, Effective Communication, etc. Thereafter, aspirants are taught law subjects such as Law of Torts, Family Law, Constitutional Law, Property Law, Company Law, Administrative Law, Civil Law, Criminal Law, and the likes. Bachelor of Business Administration & Bachelor of Legislative Law (BA LLB) law The maximum duration within which an aspirant must complete their BBA LLB course is eight years. As part of an integrated BBA LLB course, aspirants are taught various topics by means of classroom teaching, case studies and moot courts. : Also Read All about Law as a Career Popular Law Specializations Refer to the table below to know the popular law specializations: List of Law Specialization Company Law Business Law Corporate Law Criminal Law BBA LLB Eligibility Criteria Aspirants meet the eligibility criteria for BBA LLB course if they have completed their Class 12 from a recognised board. Apart from this, most law colleges/institutes also state that candidates are eligible to apply for a BBA LLB course if they secure a minimum of 45 percent aggregate at their Class 12 level (for SC/ ST category candidates the range of this minimum marks percentage is 40 percent). There is no specific age limit criteria that candidates need to fulfill in order to apply for BBA LLB course admissions. BBA LLB Entrance Exams Most popular law colleges/universities in India conduct an entrance exam in order to shortlist aspirants for admission in BBA LLB course offered by them. Some popular subjects/ topics from which questions are asked in BBA LLB entrance exams are General Knowledge, English, Elementary Mathematics (Numerical Ability), Logical Reasoning and Legal Aptitude. Some key skills that aspirants should possess in order to perform well in the BBA LLB course are listed below: Fluency Ability to work long hours Good judgment of situation/people Hard-working Interest in Research Intellectual Convincing power Clarity of speech Objectivity Persuasiveness Confidence Integrity Ability to assimilate and analyse facts Good presentation skills Planning to study Law? However, some common topics that aspirants are taught as part of their BBA LLB course curriculum are listed below: BBA LLB Course Syllabus English Principles and Practices of Business Management Quantitative Techniques Law of Torts Special Contract Criminal Law Jurisprudence (Legal Method, Indian Legal System and Basic Theory of Law) Constitutional Law Constitutional Law Fundamentals of Business Organisation Family Law Family Law Accounting Effective Communication Skills Business and Managerial Communication Managerial Economics Environmental Law Banking Law Organisational Behaviour Principles of Taxation Law Insurance Law Elective Subject: Sanskrit, German, French, Spanish, Hindi, Marathi Human Resources and Total Quality Management Law of Evidence Property Law Public International Law Elective Subject: Information Technology Law, International Banking and Finance Company Law Code of Civil Procedure and Limitation Act Trade in Intellectual Property Administrative Law Merger and Acquisitions International Trade and Economics Corporate Governance and Finance General Agreement on Tariff and Trade Labour and Industrial Law Drafting, Pleading and Conveyance Professional Ethics and Professional Accounting Systems UNCITRAL Model Codes Alternative Dispute Resolution Financial and Systematic Fraud BBA LLB Jobs and Recruiters After completing an integrated BBA LLB course, candidates are offered many job opportunities. Some popular job profiles that candidates can pursue after securing a BBA LLB degree are as mentioned below: Job Profile Job Description Lawyer In this job profile, one needs to advise and represent clients in civil as well as criminal cases. Lawyers present cases in the court of law and take part in all proceedings and hearings. Solicitor In such a job profile an individual usually specialises in a specific area of law like tax, litigation, family or property. Solicitors offer legal advice to private as well as commercial clients. Advocate In such a job profile one needs to do a lot of research work to gather factual data as well as physical evidence to support their claim. Apart from this, other responsibilities allocated to advocates include scrutinising and drafting contracts. Legal Advisor Candidates opting to work in such a job profile are also lawyers who specialise in a specific field of law. Legal advisors are usually hired by governments as well as large organisations/ companies. The main task of a legal advisor is to protect their clients from any legal implication or consequence. Teacher or Lecturer After completing an LLB degree candidates can also teach law at the college or university level. A: BBA LLB is a five-year integrated law course wherein students are taught subjects related to Business Administration as well as Law. Q: What are the eligibility criteria to apply for BBA LLB? A: The students applying for BBA LLB must have completed their Class 12 from a recognised board. Many law colleges/institutes also state that candidates must have secure a minimum of 45% aggregate at their Class 12 level. Q: Is there an age limit to appear for BBA LLB? A: No, there is no prescribed age limit to apply for BBA LLB. Q: Is there a concession for the reserved category students for BBA LLB? A: Yes, 5% relaxation is provided to candidates belonging to reserved categories, hence the minimum aggregate required is 40%. Q: Do I need to have any specific skillsets to be able to study BBA LLB? A: BBA LLB requires one to work for long hours. Some skills that BBA LLB aspirants must have are eye for detail, good judgement, clarity of speech, good communication, integrity, persuasivness, a bility to assimilate and analyse facts, good presentation skills, etc. Q: What are the future opportunities for BBA LLB graduates? A: BBA LLB graduates have numerous career opportunities such as Lawyers, Solicitor, Advocate, Legal Advisors, Teachers, etc. Q: What are the specializations offered for BBA LLB courses? A: BBA LLB courses have specializations such as Company Law, Business Law, Criminal Law and Corporate Law. Q: What is the average salary offered after BBA LLB? A: The average salary offered after BBA LLB in India is between Rs 2 LPA to Rs 7 LPA. It varies because of the factors such as experience, specialization, etc. Q: Which are some the entrance exams for BBA LLB admissions? A: Some of the common and popular entrance exams that students can take for BBA LLB admissions are CLAT, ULSAT, LSAT India, SLAT, MHCET Law, KLEE, etc. Q: Which are some of the top recruiters who hire BBA LLB graduates? A: Some of the top recruiters are Amarchand and Mangaldas & Suresh A Shroff & Co., AZB & Partners, Khaitan & Co., Luthra and Luthra Law Offices, Trilegal, Economics Laws Practice, Desai & Diwanji, etc. Q: Which are some of top colleges for BBA LLB in India?
