Colleges Top Courses UG Courses PG Courses Top Exams Q&A Articles What is Communication Design? Communication Design is a branch of that deals in utilising visuals that strategically convey message or express information. It is an approach taken by Communication Designers to engage their viewers and communicate the information and knowledge in the message clearly majorly through print or electronic media. Those who complete a Communication Design course, usually work in areas such as branding, advertising or web/app designing. Design Difference between Communication Design & Graphic Design Usually considered similar to , Communication Design careers involve a broader skillset as compared to the former. A is usually involved in handling one specific task of design creation. On the other hand, the job of a communication designer is not only about creating graphics, but also communicating the organisation’s message to the audience. A communication designer is involved in the complete marketing campaign from start to finish. Candidates can pursue the courses at undergraduate or postgraduate level in full-time, part-time or online modes. The duration of the courses ranges from few months (if enrolled in a short-term certificate programme) to four years (if enrolled in a degree programme). The Communication Design course fee usually ranges from Rs 65,000-19.16 lakh, depending on the type of programme and college (government or private) chosen. Is communication design the same as graphic design? A. Graphic design is a sub-set of communication design. While a graphic designer creates specific graphic elements, a communication designer uses those elements is designing a strategic communication plan or a complete end-to-end marketing campaign. Q. What is taught in communication design? A. In a communication design course, students are usually taught subjects such as Sketching; Design Process; Elements of Photo, Video and Animation; Branding & Identity Design; Information Design; Publication Design and so on. Q. What is the scope of communication design? A. Communication design as a career has good scope in India. Students after passing out from their respective undergraduate or postgraduate programmes can apply for jobs in industries such as Advertising, Animation, Publishing, Web Design and Movie. Q. What jobs can one get with communication design? A. Depending on your level of skills and qualification, you can work in job profiles such as Graphic Designer, Animator, Print Media Designer, Visual Designer, Brand & Social Media Manager, Creative Director and so on. Q. How much does a communication designer earn in India? A. As per Glassdoor, the average base salary of a communication designer in India is Rs 6 lakh per year. The salary, however, can increase significantly to upto Rs 20 lakh per annum as a person grows in skills and experience. Q. How do I become a communication designer? A. To become a successful communication designer in India you can follow the below steps: Obtain an undergraduate communication design degree Pursue a PG diploma or degree course in communication design Create an impressive portfolio Gain industry experience Q. How long does it take to become a communication designer? A. If you are pursuing a course at UG level, it will take three-four years to become a communication designer. If you wish to pursue masters, it will take you approximately four-five years to become a communication designer. Q. What skills are important to have for a communication designer? A. A communication designer should have creativity, an aesthetic sense, strong communication skills, good management skills and knowledge of all the latest software to be successful in the field. Q. Which are the popular communication design courses? A. The two most popular communication design courses at UG and PG level include BDes and MDes, respectively. Q. How can I get admission in a good communication design college in India? A. In order to secure admission in reputed communication design colleges in India, you need to sit for national-level entrance exams such as NIFT Entrance Exam, NID Entrance Exam, CEED and UCEED. Q.
