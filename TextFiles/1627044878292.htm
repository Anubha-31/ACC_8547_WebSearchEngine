India 2021 National Institutes of Technology (NITs) are premier autonomous public technical and research universities located all over India. Aspirants need to pass the JEE Main and GATE respectively to get admission into these institutions at undergraduate and postgraduate levels. The instruction medium in all NITs is English. India is home to some of the top-rated NITs such as National Institute of Technology (NIT Trichy), National Institute of Technology (NITK Surathkal), National Institute of Technology (NIT Rourkela), National Institute of Technology (NIT Warangal), National Institute of Technology (NIT Calicut), Visvesvaraya National Institute of Technology (VNIT Nagpur), Malaviya National Institute of Technology (MNIT Jaipur), National Institute of Technology (NIT Kurukshetra), National Institute of Technology (NIT Silchar), National Institute of Technology (NIT Durgapur), etc. BTech degree is the most common undergraduate degree in NITs. Few NITs also offer a five-year BArch and four-year BSc degree. NITs offer many postgraduate programmes including MTech, MBA, MSc and MCA. Some of NITs offer an MS (by research) program; the MTech and M.S. are similar to the US universities' non-thesis (course-based) and thesis (research-based) Masters programs respectively. Admissions to Masters programs in engineering are made using scores of the Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE), while those to Masters programs in science are made using scores of the Joint Admission Test to MSc (JAM). NITs also offer an unconventional BTech and MTech integrated educational program called "Dual Degree" like NIT Rourkela and NIT Agartala. NITs also offer the Doctor of Philosophy degree (PhD) as part of their doctoral education program. An degree is offered across various specialisations. Some of the most prominent ones are as follows: Engineering Civil Engineering Computer Science and Technology Chemical Engineering Computer Science and Engineering Petroleum Engineering Engineering Courses Eligibility Criteria Engineering courses can be pursued at UG, PG, Diploma and PhD levels from NITs in India. The eligibility criteria differ as per the course and branch selected by the candidate. However, given below is a broad eligibility criteria for UG, PG, Diploma and PhD Engineering courses for the reference of candidates: Candidates must have passed Class 12th from a recognised board with Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics as core subjects. They must have also secured a minimum of 60 percent aggregate score. BE/BTech: Candidates must have passed a three-year Diploma granted by the Board of Technical Examination of a State Government, with a minimum 45 percent aggregate or equivalent. BTech Lateral Entry: Candidates who have completed their BE/BTech degree with a valid score in GATE are eligible for ME/MTech degree course. ME/MTech: Aspirants must have passed Class 10/equivalent examination, with at least 35 percent in Science and Maths subjects. Diploma: Aspirants must have secured a minimum of 55 percent aggregate or 5.5 CPI in BTech/MTech or equivalent degree in relevant streams. PhD: Apart from merit-based admission, many top-ranked Engineering colleges in India also accept scores of national and state-level entrance exams including etc., for Engineering admissions. Students investing in higher education yield long-term benefits. Some students can opt for programmes like MTech / ME and MS while some go on to do business administration courses like MBA.
