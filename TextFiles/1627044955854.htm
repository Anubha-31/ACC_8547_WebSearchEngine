The career specialisations offered for MBBS students in India include Medicine, General Surgery, Surgical Technology and Paramedical. A. Q6. How to know the authenticity of an MBBS course in India? Before taking admission to any medical college, check whether the college is recognised by the Medical Council of India (MCI). Also, check if the college/ university is approved by the University Grants Commission (UGC) and Indian Medical Council (IMC). A. Q7. Can we join MBBS without NEET? No, to join the MBBS course you need to write the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET). Even if you want to opt for a management quota seat, you have to secure the qualifying percentile in NEET. A. Q8. Can I do MBBS after Class 12? Yes, to join an MBBS course you need to complete 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology and English. You must also secure a minimum aggregate of 50% in the core subjects (PCB). A. Q9. What is the total duration of the MBBS degree course? To complete the MBBS degree course, you need to complete two stages: 4.5 years of academics and 1 year of internship. Hence, the total duration of an MBBS degree course is 5.5 years. A. Q10. Can one use "Dr." after completing MBBS? Yes, once you clear the final examination or qualifying examination of MBBS, you need to complete a compulsory rotatory internship of 1 year from a recognised institution/ hospital. On successful completion of the internship, you can officially add "Dr" to your name. A. Q11. Can MBBS doctors do surgery? Yes, an MBBS is a graduate in Surgery, hence, he/ she is licenced to perform surgeries.
