Later in 1985, the Railway Services Commissions were renamed and restructured as the Railway Recruitment Boards. In the year 1998, all the RRBs came under the umbrella organisation Railway Recruitment Control Board (RRCB), which directly works under the Ministry of Railway (Railway Board). RRBs are responsible for the recruitment of personnel at various positions. They coordinate, recruit through a centralised recruitment advertisement and conduct online computer-based test (CBT) across the nation. The candidates who fulfil all the eligibility conditions are supposed to fill the application form because, in the absence of that, the candidature of the candidate will be liable to cancel at advance stage of examination. All the original documents will be verified at the stage of document verification and in absence of any required document, the candidate will lose his candidature. Candidates waiting for final results of prescribed educational qualification should not apply. Nationality (i) A candidate must be either: a. a citizen of India, or b. a subject of Nepal, or c. a subject of Bhutan, or d. a Tibetan refugee who came over to India, before the 1st January 1962 with the intention of permanently settling in India, or e. a person of Indian origin who has migrated from Pakistan, Burma, Sri Lanka, East African Countries of Kenya, Uganda, the United Republic of Tanzania, Zambia, Malawi, Zaire, Ethiopia and Vietnam with the intention of permanently settling in India. Provided that a candidate belonging to categories (b), (c), (d) and (e) above shall be a person in whose favour a certificate of eligibility has been issued by the Government of India. The person may be selected and recommended but the appointment letter will be issued to him only after he gets such certificate form the Government of India. The RRBs issue centralised notification and invites application from the candidates for the nearest RRB. Later the RRBs also issues the Application status of the candidates. Application status is issued so that the candidates can confirm the acceptance of their application for the RRB JE exam. Respective RRBs had released the RRB JE application status related to their acceptance and rejection for the Computer-based test (CBT) on March 26, 2019. The application status were released after the scrutiny process of applications were complete. The candidates were given reasons for the rejection of their application. The status checking window was opened till March 31, 2019. RRB JE Recruitment Process RRB JE exam is conducted in four stages starting from Stage I Computer Based Test( CBT) to the Medical test. The selection process has the following steps: Filling up the Application Form The RRB JE Recruitment application will be invited from the candidates after the notification is released. The candidates are given a time period of 25 to 30 days to fill the application form online. For the RRB JE 2019 the application fees The application fee was Rs.500 for the unreserved category and Rs.250 for the SC / ST / Ex-Servicemen / PwBDs / Female / Transgender / Minorities / Economically backward class. It was also mentioned that this examination fees will be refunded fully to the reserved category candidates when they appear in the CBT-1 exam. The unreserved category candidates will receive Rs.400 back in their account on appearing in the CBT-1. The candidates were allowed to modify their application form twice only. For each modification in the RRB JE application form, the candidates were required to pay a fee Rs.100. Issue of RRB JE CBT -1 Admit Card The RRB JE admit card is released for each stage of the exam separately and the candidates are required to download the admit card and present it at the exam centre. The other government ID proofs are also required with the adit card and can be asked at the time of exam by the competent authority. The RRB JE admit card is released to all the candidates whose application were accepted by the respective RRBs. The admit card for the RRB JE CBT-1 was issue 4 days before the CBT-1 exam. For the RRB JE recruitment exam 2019, the CBT -1 was scheduled to be conducted from May 22, 2019 but was later rescheduled to June 26 to June 28. RRB JE CBT -1 Exam RRB JE CBT-1 exam is the first stage of the selection process. It is a computer-based test conducted to filter out the candidates for CBT-2. The RRB JE 2019 CBT -1 was conducted from June 26 to June 28, 2019. Approximately 15 Lakh candidates appeared in the exam. The CBT-1 is an online objective type test and the duration of this test is 90 Minutes. There were 100 questions in the CBT-1 and each question was of 1 mark. The candidates who appeared in the RRB JE CBT-1 were entitled to get the refund amount as mentioned above. The score of CBT-1 was decided after the normalisation of marks done in the manner defined in the RRB JE Notification. The raw marks obtained by the candidates are converted into normalised marks to give them all a level playing field. The raw marks for single session paper and normalized marks for the multi-session paper were used for computing Merit Index, which is a common benchmark for generating merit for candidates from different exam groups. Normalisation Formula for RRB JE Recruitment For the multisession papers, a suitable normalization is applied to take into account any variation in the difficulty levels of the question papers across different sessions. Calculation of marks was made up to 5 decimal points. The merit of the CBT-1 is calculated based on these normalised scores of the candidates. RRB JE Result for CBT-1 RRB JE Result for CBT-1 was declared based on the normalised marks and around two lakh candidates were qualified for the RRB CBT-2. RRB JE CBT-2 Admit Card The RRB JE admit card is issued for each stage of the exam. The candidates can download the admit card four days before the exam. The RRB JE Admit card was first issued on August 24, 2019, but later the exam was postponed and the e-call letter for the CBT-2 was finally issued on September 15, 2019. RRB JE CBT-2 Exam The RRB JE CBT-2 exam is the second of the four stages of the recruitment process. This is a crucial and decisive stage for the final selection. Around 2 lakh candidates appeared in the RRB JE CBT-2 2019. The RRB JE CBT-2 is also an objective type test having 150 questions and time duration of 120 minutes. There was a negative marking of 1/3 in the RRB JE CBT-2 also. RRB JE Result for CBT-2 There will be normalisation of marks obtained by the candidates in the RRB JE CBT-2 exam. The RRB JE Result for CBT-2 marks the completion of RRB JE Stage 2. In addition to above, extra candidates will also be called for the document verification to maintain a standby list of 50% as given in the official notification. RRB JE Document Verification (DV) This is the penultimate stage of the RRB JE Recruitment process. All the candidates qualified in the RRB JE CBT-2 will be called for their original document verification. The original document of the candidates is verified in accordance with the claims made in the RRB JE online application form. In case of any discrepancy found during the DV Stage, the candidature of the candidate stands cancelled. RRB JE Medical Examination After the Document verification stage, the qualified candidates need to qualify the medical test as per the given medical standards for each exam group in the official notification. The Medical examination is conducted by the Railway Administration to ensure the medical fitness of the qualified candidates. Candidates who have undergone Lasik surgery or any other surgery procedure to correct refractory error are not eligible for the post having Medical Standards A3, B1 and B2. Different Medical standards shall apply to Ex-Servicemen. After successfully qualifying the medical examination, the candidates are recommended to the service by declaring the final result and issuing them the appointment letter.
