Law is a career stream that candidates pursue at undergraduate (UG), postgraduate (PG) or doctorate (PhD) level to practice the legal profession. Law as a profession offers a plethora of career avenues for students to explore and conquer. Preferred specialisation in Law is either Criminal or Civil. However, these days other branches are also gaining popularity such as Cyber Law, Patent Law and as well as Corporate Law. Students looking forward to a career in Law can opt from UG, PG, integrated and PhD degrees. Students looking forward to a career in Law have a wide range of options in UG, PG, PhD and dual/integrated degrees. Students can choose to pursue courses such as BA LLB, BBA LLB, BSc LLB, BCom LLB, LLM and PhD in a variety of Law branches/ specialisations at Law colleges in Kolkata such as: Corporate Law Criminal Law Constitutional Law Cyber Law Finance Information Technology Law Intellectual Property Law International / International Trade Law Labor and Employment Law Business Law Candidates can pursue degree, Diploma and certificate at UG, PG, and PhD levels. Law Courses Candidates can obtain either an integrated Law degree such as BA LLB, BBA LLB, BSc LLB, BCom LLB, etc., after clearing Class 12th or a regular LLB degree after clearing graduation. The duration of an integrated LLB degree is usually five years and that of a regular LLB is three years. Degree law courses: Candidates can pursue Diploma courses in Law at both UG and PG levels. The duration of Diploma or PG diploma Law courses usually ranges from one to three years. Diploma law courses: Certificate law courses are short-term programmes that range from 2-6 months. Certificate law courses: Law Courses Eligibility Criteria Law courses can be pursued at UG, PG and PhD levels. The eligibility criteria differs as per the course and branch selected by the candidate. UG level : For admission in LLB (3 years) course, aspirants need to graduate from a recognised university. Also, for admission in LLM course candidates need to possess an LLB or equivalent degree from a recognised university. PG level The candidate must have passed postgraduation in Law in regular mode from a recognised university with a minimum of 55% aggregate.
