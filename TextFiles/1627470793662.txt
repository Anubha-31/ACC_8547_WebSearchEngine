What is Mechatronics Engineering?



Mechatronics engineering is a multidisciplinary study of the engineering/technology field that deals with the study of electronics engineering, mechanical engineering, telecommunications, computer engineering, system engineering, and control engineering. Mechatronics engineering is a connection amongst technicians and engineers and gradually work from origination of a project to the culmination of the undertaking.
The study module in the Mechatronic Engineering programme comprises a wide range of education in the basic standards and principles of Mechatronics, computer, and mechanical engineering. Mechatronics Engineering mainly focuses on various parts of mechatronics such as modeling, sensors, controllers, real-time computer interfacing, and actuators. After finishing the Mechatronics Engineering degree, candidates can work on different aspects of technology such as control systems, artificial intelligence (AI) and expert systems, robotics, transportation, and computer integrated manufacturing systems and much more.
Many public and private universities/colleges in India have Mechatronics Engineering courses and specialisation for students. After a successful completion of the Mechatronic Engineering undergraduate programme, there will be plenty of varied job opportunities have emerged for those trained in this domain.




Skilled mechatronic engineers are in high demand in the field of implementing, designing and operating internet control of machines, engine management systems and autonomous robots. If a candidate is planning for a career in mechatronic engineering then he/she should begin with an undergraduate programme followed by a master's degree. Mechatronics Engineering Eligibility Criteria
Engineering aspirants who aim to pursue the Mechatronic Engineering programme must fulfil the following eligibility criteria:


Bachelor's Degree:
In order to get into undergraduate level Mechatronic Engineering, an applicant must have
passed Class 12th with a minimum of 50% of marks. The candidates must have studied Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics are the subjects in 12th.

If candidates want to pursue a degree in on the IITs exclusively, it is mandatory to have passed in the Joint Entrance Examinations JEE Main.

In order to pursue a post-graduate level Mechatronic Engineering course, he/she must have a bachelor's degree in Engineering or equivalent engineering branches.
Master's Degree:

If candidates want to pursue a degree in on the IITs exclusively, it is mandatory to have passed in the GATE.

FAQs Regarding Mechatronics Engineering

Q: What is the career scope for Mechatronics engineer?
A: An Mechatronics engineer will easily get the job as a Computer System Analyst, Researcher, Supervisor, Robotics Test Engineer and Application Engineer after completing the degree/diploma.

Q: Is it possible to pursue Mechatronics Engineering in distance learning method?
A: Yes, it is possible to pursue this program in distance learning.

Q: Is Mechatronics Engineering easy?
A: It is only a state of mind and depends on the level of effort we put in to do something. Any field is easy if you try and hardest when you don't try.

Q: What is the fee structure of the Mechatronics Engineering courses?
A: The fee of the Mechatronics Engineering course varies from institute to institute. The total fee ranges from Rs 1 lakh to Rs 4 lakh.

Q: How do I ensure that I am enrolling into a genuine institute?
A: While shortlisting colleges, candidates have to ensure that the institute is approved and recognized by the Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA). Without the DGCA approval, the licence would not be considered valid.

Q: Is Mechatronics engineering a good career?
A: Yes, a mechatronics career can be a rewarding one because Mechatronics is a multi-faceted field. Candidates can learn about motors, control systems, software development for making things autonomous, sensors and how to use them, electronic components and embedded hardware, kinematics and dynamics of systems and CAD.

Q: How difficult is the entrance exam for the Mechatronics Engineering course?
A: The difficulty level can be marked as moderate to tough.

Q: Do the institutes offering Mechatronics Engineering program provide placement support as well?
A: Yes, the institutes offer placement support upon the completion of the courses.

Q: What is the job role of the Mechatronics engineer consists of?
A: Mechatronics Engineering graduates are involved in the problems related computers, software system, actively managing and driving the technology evaluation of sales process and use their skills in electric, electrical and mechanical systems and assist senior level engineers in the production and development of automated equipment.

Q: What is the average salary of Mechatronics Engineering?
A: The salary would vary on the basis of factors like rank, years of experience, recruiting company and overall performance. On average, the salary may vary from Rs 2 lakh to Rs 6 lakh and above.
