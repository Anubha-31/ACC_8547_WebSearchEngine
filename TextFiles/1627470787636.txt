There are over 100 Distance/Correspondence Diploma colleges in India that offer Distance/Correspondence Diploma course, out of these nearly 80% of colleges are private, over 20% are government.
Diploma is a type of recognition issued by an educational institution to a person on the completion of an educational course successfully. It is a short-term course of 1 to 2 years usually awarded for professional/vocational courses. While pursuing diploma, a person is trained in a particular field. It is not necessary that the institute or the university offering diploma education be UGC or AICTE approved. It can be studied full-time or from distance/correspondence.
In India, there are total 110 Distance/Correspondence Diploma college. Some of these are:

Study Scope after Distance/Correspondence Diploma
After completion of Distance/Correspondence Diploma, aspirants can apply for admission to regular or distance degree programme. Aspirants holding Diploma in engineering can apply for admission to BE/BTech, admission to BE/BTech programme is done through and various state level engineering entrance examination such as UPSEE, WBJEE, AP EAMCET, TS EAMCET, KCET, etc. However, some institutes also conduct their entrance exam to provide admission to the eligible students.
JEE Main
Similarly, students possessing Diploma in medical field can apply for admission to BPharma, BSc, MBBS, etc. is conducted every year for admission to MBBS whereas there are various state level entrance examinations for admission to BPharma and BSc.
