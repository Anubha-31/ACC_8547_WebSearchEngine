What is UCEED Exam? Undergraduate Common Entrance Examination for Design (UCEED) is conducted by under the guidance of the UCEED-CEED Implementation Committee every year to offer admission to eligible candidates in Bachelor of Design () programmes offered at colleges accepting UCEED scores. IIT Bombay BDes UCEED is an online examination of three hours duration that is typically conducted in the month of January every year. Merely appearing for the UCEED exam or securing a spot in the UCEED rank list does not guarantee admission in BDes programmes. Candidates who qualify for UCEED need to separately apply for admission as per the procedure prescribed by IIT Bombay. Admissions in BDes are done on the basis of merit in each category. The merit list is prepared on the basis of scores obtained by candidates in the written entrance exam. Aspirants can go through UCEED statistics and develop an idea of the high competition levels in the design entrance exam. Aspirants meet UCEED eligibility, if they fulfill the below-mentioned criteria: Age Limit Candidates should have been born on or after October 1, 1996, if they belong to the General/ Open/ EWS/ OBC-NCL category. However, aspirants from SC/ ST/ PwD category should have been born on or after October 1, 1991. Number of Attempts Candidates who meet the UCEED age criteria can take the exam a maximum of two times and that too in consecutive years. Candidates need to possess UCEED rank to secure admission in the BDes programme at its participating colleges. UCEED score is valid for one year and is applicable for admission only in the corresponding academic year. Qualifying Exam Aspirants who have cleared their or equivalent (qualifying) examination in 2020 OR are appearing for the exams in 2021 fulfill UCEED eligibility 2021. Candidates who have appeared for their Class XII (or equivalent) examination the first time in 2019 or earlier are not eligible to appear for the UCEED exam. UCEED 2021 Admission Process UCEED Selection Process 2021 Candidates need to ensure that they complete each and every step that forms a part of the UCEED admission process. Candidates are informed that merely securing a UCEED rank does not guarantee admission. Thus, candidates need to fulfill UCEED eligibility criteria, clear the entrance exam, and thereafter take part in the counselling round in order to secure admission through UCEED 2021. Further, candidates can go through the steps they need to follow as part of the UCEED selection process. Step 1: UCEED Application Form The application form for UCEED is to be filled in three steps. Firstly, aspirants have to register for the exam online. Next, candidates need to complete filling the online application form. Lastly, candidates have to pay UCEED application fee in the online mode. UCEED 2021 application fee is Rs 3,000 (for General and OBC candidates) and Rs 1,500 (for SC, ST, PwD, and female candidates). to take place from September 9 to October 17, 2020. UCEED 2021 exam FAQs on UCEED 2021 Q: When will UCEED 2021 counselling be held? A: UCEED counselling 2021 is underway. The first UCEED 2021 seat allotment result was announced on April 10. The second UCEED seat allotment result was out on May 10. The third UCEED seat allotment result was out on June 10. The first supplementary round will be held on July 30. Q: How many times is UCEED conducted in a year? A: UCEED is conducted once a year typically in the month of January by IIT Bombay. UCEED 2021 was conducted on January 17. Q: How many times can I appear for the UCEED exam? A: Candidates can appear for UCEED for maximum two times and that too in consecutive years. UCEED score for admission is valid for one year only. Q: Is UCEED 2021 being conducted online or offline? A: UCEED 2021 was conducted as an online and offline exam. Part A of UCEED 2021 was held in online mode as a computer-based test and Part B of the exam had to be attempted on a sheet provided inside the exam hall. Q: What is the format of the UCEED question paper? A: UCEED is a three-hour duration test in which candidates are evaluated out of 300 marks. The paper usually comprises 68 objective questions that are spread across three sections – Section A (NAT - Numerical Answer Type), Section B (MSQ - Multiple Select Questions), and Section C (MCQ - Multiple Choice Questions). Part B of the UCEED exam will contain one sketching related question. Check the complete UCEED pattern . Q: Is there any subjective question on drawing in the UCEED question paper? A: Yes, the UCEED question paper contains one subjective question to test the drawing skills of candidates. Aspirants need to read this paper on their computer screen and attempt it on a sheet provided inside their exam hall. Q: What is the use of UCEED exam? A: Undergraduate Common Entrance Examination for Design or UCEED is conducted by IIT Bombay in order to shortlist aspirants for admission in the BDes course offered at Industrial Design Centre (IDC), IIT Guwahati, IIITDM Jabalpur, IIT Hyderabad as well as other colleges that accept UCEED scores for admission . A: Candidates from any stream can apply for the UCEED exam. However, as mentioned in UCEED 2021 eligibility criteria, candidates who are from the Science stream and have studied subjects like Physics , Chemistry, and Mathematics are eligible to apply for admission at IIT Bombay, IIT Guwahati , IIT Hyderabad, and IIITDM Jabalpur . Part A of the exam consists of Numerical Answer Type (NAT) Questions, Multiple Select Questions (MSQ), and Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ) whereas Part B consists of a drawing question. Questions in the UCEED exam are from topics such as – Visualisation and Spatial Ability, Observation and Design Sensitivity, Environmental and Social Awareness, Analytical and Logical Reasoning, Language and Creativity as well as Design Thinking and Problem Solving.
