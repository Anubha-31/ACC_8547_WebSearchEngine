The Bachelor of Arts - Bachelor of Legislative Law (BA LLB) course is an integrated degree that the law aspirants can pursue right after qualifying their Class 12th examinations. The law degree is an integration of Arts and Law subjects and is of five-years duration. Therefore, as part of the BA LLB course, the candidates study subjects including Economics, History, Political Science, Sociology along with law subjects like Civil Law, Criminal Law, Labour Law, Tax Law, Administrative Law, Corporate Law, Patent Law etc. The course-curriculum of BA LLB degree is such that law, as well as, the arts subjects, are included in each semester. Moreover, candidates are taught about law case studies, moot courts, law internships, seminars and interactions with retired judges from High courts and the Supreme Court of India. : law Also Read All about Law as a Career Popular Law Specializations Company Law Business Law Corporate Law Criminal Law BA LLB Eligibility Criteria Candidates seeking admission to BA LLB course need to meet the eligibility criteria as mentioned by the law schools and universities in India including, academic qualification, minimum qualifying marks and age limit. if they have completed their 10+2 from a recognised board. Apart from this, most popular law colleges also fix a minimum marks requirement in order to be eligible to apply for the BA LLB course offered by them. By means of this criteria, candidates belonging to General/ OBC/ Specially abled person categories need to secure at least 45% marks at their 10+2 level in order to pursue a BA LLB course and the percentage fixed for SC/ ST category is 40%. The states no minimum or maximum age limit criteria is required for admissions to BA LLB course or five-year integrated law courses. Bar Council of India (BCI) BA LLB Skillset BA LLB course requires aspirants to be hardworking and extremely thorough in their subject. Some key skills that aspirants should possess in order to perform well in BA LLB course are listed below: Fluency Clarity of speech Ability to work long hours Objectivity Good judgement of situation/people Persuasiveness Hard working Confidence Interest in Research Integrity Intellect Ability to assimilate and analyse facts Convincing power Good presentation skills Planning to study Law? Some popular job profiles that candidates can pursue after securing a BA LLB degree are as mentioned below: Job Profile Job Description Lawyer In this job profile, one needs to advise and represent clients in civil as well as criminal cases. Lawyers present cases in the court of law and take part in all proceedings and hearings. Solicitor In such a job profile an individual usually specialises in a specific area of law like tax, litigation, family or property. Solicitors offer legal advice to private as well as commercial clients. Advocate In such a job profile one needs to do a lot of research work to gather factual data as well as physical evidence to support their claim. Apart from this, other responsibilities allocated to advocates include scrutinising and drafting contracts. Legal Advisor Candidates opting to work in such a job profile are also lawyers who specialise in a specific field of law. Legal advisors are usually hired by governments as well as large organisations/ companies. The main task of a legal advisor is to protect their clients from any legal implication or consequence. Teacher or Lecturer After completing an LLB degree candidates can also teach law at the college or university level. Q. What is BA LLB? A. BA LLB or Bachelor of Legislative Law is an undergraduate degree, which focuses on integrating knowledge regarding law and other subjects to the candidates. Students are also introduced to law case studies, which will help them practice for their career in the law field. Q. Is BA LLB better or LLB? A. BA LLB is an integrated 5-year programme, where the student has to study LLB along with other Arts subjects. However, LLB is a 3-year programme that focuses solely on the study of law. Q. I have a Science background in 12th standard. Can I apply for BA LLB? A. Yes. Students of any stream can apply for BA LLBprovided that they meet the basic eligibility criteria. Q. Is BA LLB merit or entrance based? A. For BA LLB admissions, the students will have to attempt the respective entrance examination of the institute that they wish to apply for admissions. Q. What are the entrance exams for BA LLB? A. Some of the national level entrance examinations for BA LLB are CLAT and LSAT. There are many state-level entrance examinations too which the candidates can apply for based on their eligibility. Q. What is the scope of BA LLB? A. Students who graduate with a BA LLB degree can start working in the law field. They can work as a lawyer, advocate, solicitor and more. They can also pursue higher education such as LLM or even MBA. Q. What is the salary of BA LLB graduates? A. The salary of BA LLB graduates will differ as per the field they are working in or the company or organization that they are working at. However, the average salary falls between INR 3 to 6 Lakhs per annum. Q. Is CLAT necessary for BA LLB? A. Yes. CLAT is necessary for BA LLB admissions. Q. Who are the top recruiters for BA LLB graduates? A. There are many companies who recruit BA LLB graduates; however, some of the top recruiters are Amarchand & Mangaldas & Suresh A Shroff & Co., J Sagar Associates, Trilegal and more. Q. Do I need to study Political Science in BA LLB? A. Yes. Students will also be required to study Political Science along with some other Arts subjects in BA LLB. Q. Which is the best college for BA LLB? A. According to India Today 2020 Rankings, the National Law School of India University is the top-ranked college for BA LLB. Popular B.A. LL.B.
