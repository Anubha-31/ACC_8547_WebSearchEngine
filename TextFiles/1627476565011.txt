Delhi/NCR covers entire National Capital Region (NCR) comprising the neighbouring cities of Delhi – Gurgaon, Ghaziabad, Noida, Greater Noida, Faridabad, Sonepat, along with Delhi. Delhi, the capital city of India, is among the top MBA destinations in the country. MBA Colleges in Delhi/NCR offer quality education with high placements. MBA is one of the most popular post-graduate programmes in Delhi. The two-year programme is a gateway to a plethora of job opportunities in the corporate world.
MBA in Delhi is broadly offered by three types of MBA colleges, namely – Delhi University MBA colleges like FMS, DSE, SRCC; MBA colleges under Central government and other state universities such as IIFT Delhi, GGSIPU (IP University) Delhi, Ambedkar University Delhi, JNU Delhi, O P Jindal University Sonepat. AICTE-approved PGDM Colleges in Delhi/NCR like MDI Gurgaon, IMI New Delhi, IMT Ghaziabad, FORE School of Management Delhi, LBSIM New Delhi, BIMTECH Greater Noida among other top PGDM colleges. All these management institutes make Delhi/NCR a preferred MBA destination. An MBA professional must possess qualities like excellent communication, leadership, problem-solving, management, research-oriented, analytical thinking, eye for detail, goal-oriented & ability to work under pressure, strong mathematical skills, etc.
In Delhi/NCR, is offered across various specialisations. Some of the most prominent ones are as follows:
MBA degree

Finance
Sales and Marketing
Human Resources
International Business
IT and Systems
Operations
Business Analytics
Retail
Supply Chain
Entrepreneurship
The eligibility criteria for MBA course may differ from college to college. However, the basic eligibility criteria for MBA is as follows:

Candidates must have passed graduation in any discipline or equivalent from a recognised university as a basic eligibility criteria for MBA (full-time).
Most of the institutes follow minimum score criteria in graduation which is 50 percent in average or equivalent. For reserved category students, the minimum score is 45 percent in aggregate.
Final-year graduation candidates are also eligible to apply for MBA, provided they present the proof of completion of graduation degree within the duration specified by the institute.
