SSC CGL is a national-level exam conducted by the Staff Selection Commission (SSC) to shortlist candidates for Group-B and -C posts in various ministries/departments/organisations of the Government of India. Every year, about 30 lakh candidates fill the SSC CGL application form and about 15 lakh aspirants appear for the exam. The conducts the exam in four stages called tiers. The first two tiers of the Combined Graduate Level (CGL) exam are conducted in online mode. The next two tiers of the exam are held in offline mode. Check . The SSC CGL application form was released by Staff Selection Commission on December 29, 2020. How to Fill SSC CGL Application Form? Candidates could fill on the official website of the Commission in two parts - registration and application. The steps to fill the application form are given below. SSC CGL 2021 application form Step 1: Visit the official website of SSC - ssc.nic.in. Step 2: Click on the 'New User? Register Now 'link. Step 3: Fill the SSC CGL registration form. Step 4: Login to the registered account using registration number (generated at the time of registration) and password (set at the time of registration). Step 5: Fill all details asked in the SSC CGL application form. Step 6: Upload scanned images of photo and signature and other important documents, if applicable. Step 7: Review the SSC online form carefully and click on the Final Submit button. Step 8: Pay SSC CGL application fee of Rs 100 in online or offline mode. SSC CGL Photo and Signature Scanned Images Specifications Candidates had to upload the scanned images of the photo and signature in JPEG/JPG format as per the specifications given below: Photo size 20-50 KB Signature size 10-20 KB SSC CGL Application Fee Candidates could pay the SSC CGL application fee of Rs 100 in online or offline mode via SBI challan/BHIM UPI/internet banking/credit card/debit card. However, all females and candidates belonging to reserved categories such as Scheduled Caste (SC), Scheduled Tribe (ST), Persons with Disability (PwD) and Ex-Servicemen (ESM) were exempted from the application fee payment. See here: SSC CGL Answer Key of Previous Years SSC CGL 2021 Recruitment Process SSC CGL recruitment process 2021 includes the following stages/tiers: Firstly, candidates need to fill the SSC CGL application form in online mode on the official website of the commission. The SSC CGL application fee is Rs 100. SSC CGL application form: : All candidates whose applications are accepted by Staff Selection Commission can then download their SSC CGL admit card for tier-I exam from the official regional websites of the commission as per the prescribed schedule. SSC CGL Tier-I admit card : SSC CGL tier-I is a computer-based exam that contains objective-type Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) of 200 marks. SSC CGLTier-I exam : SSC CGL result is declared by Staff Selection Commission on its official website. The result contains the names and/or roll numbers of candidates qualifying the tier-I exam. Candidates clearing the tier-I paper are called to appear for tier-II exam. SSC CGL Tier-I result : All qualifying candidates can download their SSC CGL admit card for tier-II from the official regional websites. The admit card is typically released about one week before the exam. SSC CGL Tier-II admit card : SSC CGL tier-II is again a computer-based exam that is divided into four papers. Each paper contains 100 MCQs of 200 marks. SSC CGL Tier-II exam : SSC CGL result for tier-II is declared on the official website of the commission in the form of a merit list. Candidates clearing the tier-II exam are called to appear for tier-III paper. SSC CGL Tier-II result : All qualifying candidates again need to download their CGL admit card from the regional SSC websites as per the prescribed schedule. SSC CGL Tier-III admit card : SSC CGL tier-III is a pen-and-paper based exam that is descriptive in nature. SSC CGL Tier-III exam : Similar to tier-I and -II result, candidates can check SSC CGL result for tier-III also in online mode on the official website. Candidates clearing the tier-III exam need to appear for the final stage – tier-IV. SSC CGL Tier-III result : Like admit card for other tiers, SSC CGL tier-IV admit card is also available for download on the regional websites of Staff Selection Commission, SSC CGL Tier-IV admit card : In tier-IV, SSC conducts either a computer proficiency test, skill test or document verification, as per the post applied by the candidate. SSC CGL tier-IV is only a qualifying paper. It means that marks scored by candidates in tier-IV are not calculated at the time of declaration of final result. SSC CGL Tier-IV exam : Final SSC CGL result is announced based on candidates' performance in tier-I, tier-II and tier-III exams. All qualifying candidates are then allotted Group-B and -C posts in various ministries/departments/organisations of the Government of India. A: The first two tiers of CGL exam are held in online mode and the next two tiers of the exam are conducted in offline mode. Q: How many stages does SSC CGL exam contain? A: SSC CGL exam contains four stages - Tier-I (online exam), Tier-II (online exam), Tier-III (offline exam) and Tier-IV (computer proficiency test/skill test/document verification). Q: What was the application fee for SSC CGL exam? A: The SSC CGL application fee was Rs 100. However, women candidates of all categories and reserved category candidates were exempted from paying the application fee. Q: Is it mandatory to appear for all the tiers of SSC CGL exam? A: Yes, candidates need to appear for all the tiers of SSC CGL in order to qualify the exam. Q: Is there any negative marking in SSC CGL exam? A: Yes, there is a negative marking of 0.50 marks in tier-I, tier-III, and tier-IV papers. Also, there is a negative marking of 0.25 marks in tier-II paper of CGL exam. Q: What is the minimum eligibility criteria to apply for SSC CGL exam? A: Candidates who have completed graduation from a recognised university are eligible for the SSC CGL exam. Q: How many SSC CGL vacancies are released every year? A: SSC CGL exam is conducted for a good number of vacancies every year. For SSC CGL 2019-20 exam, total 9,488 vacancies had been released by the commission. Q: For which posts is SSC CGL exam conducted? A: SSC CGL exam is conducted for various Group-B and Group -C posts available in different ministries/departments/organisations of the Government of India. Q: How many times is SSC CGL conducted in a year? A: Each stage (tier-I, II, III and IV) of SSC CGL exam is conducted once a year. Q: Was there an age limit to appear for SSC CGL exam? A: SSC CGL age limit differs as per the post applied. However, candidates should be minimum 18 years in age to apply for any SSC CGL post.
