Aspirants belonging to reserved categories such as OBC (non-creamy layer)/SC/ST/PwD are eligible to apply if they have secured a minimum of 50% aggregate in their Master’s degree or equivalent exam. Aspirants who are appearing for their Master’s degree final year exam and whose exam result is awaited can also apply for APSET 2019 exam. But such aspirants will be offered provisional admission and they need to submit their exam result details at the time of admission. Candidates who possess a PhD degree and who have completed their Master’s level examination by September 19, 1991 (irrespective of the date when the result is declared) will be eligible for 5% relaxation in aggregate marks (that is, from 55% to 50%) for appearing in APSET exam. The official website informs that, “Candidates are advised to appear in the subject of their post-graduation only. The candidates, whose post-graduation subject is not covered in the list of subjects may appear in a related subject”. There is no upper age limit for candidates applying for the post of Assistant Professor. The APSET stands for Andhra Pradesh State Eligibility Test which is conducted to determine the eligibility of candidates for Assistant Professors or Lecturers in the colleges and universities of the state. Q. Who conducts the APSET exam? A. Andhra University, Vishakhapatnam conducts the APSET exam on behalf of the Government of Andhra Pradesh. Q. Is APSET a recruitment exam? A. No, APSET is not a recruitment exam. Candidates who clear the exam become eligible to apply for the recruitment of Assistant Professors or Lecturers in the state colleges and universities. Q. In how many subjects the APSET exam is conducted? A. The APSET exam is conducted for a total of 30 subjects. Q. When will APSET notification 2021 release? A. The APSET notification 2021 is expected to release in the second half of 2021. Q. How to appear for the APSET 2021 exam? A. Candidates who want to appear for the APSET 2021 exam need to fill the online application form, once released by Andhra University. Q. What is the APSET examination process? A. The APSET exam process comprises various stages such as the release of APSET notification, filling of application form, APSET admit card, the conduct of APSET exam and release of the result. Q. What is the APSET exam pattern? A. The APSET exam pattern comprises Paper-I and Paper-II. In Paper-I, a total of 50 MCQs are asked and in Paper-II 100 MCQs are asked. Paper-I is common for all candidates and Paper-II is conducted in 30 subjects. Q. What are the minimum qualifying marks for the APSET exam? A. The minimum qualifying marks for the APSET exam are 40 per cent for the General category and 35 per cent for OBC (NCL)/SC/ST/PwD category. Q. Is there a negative marking in the APSET exam? A. No, there is no negative marking in the APSET exam. Q. Is the APSET exam conducted online? A. No, the APSET exam is not conducted online. The exam is held in offline mode and candidates need to mark their answers on the OMR sheet. Q. What is the subject of APSET Paper-I? A. The APSET Paper-I is of general nature. The questions are framed to test candidates' reasoning ability, comprehension, divergent thinking and general awareness. Q. When is the APSET result declared? A. The APSET result is generally declared within two months of the exam. APSET 2020 Contact Information Andhra University, Visakhapatnam - 530 003, Andhra Pradesh, India.
