BLS LLB or Bachelor of Legal Science & Bachelor of Legislative Law is a five years integrated course in which aspirants can pursue right after their Class 12. BLS LLB course is similar to and BBA LLB course in the view that candidates receive two separate degrees on completion of the course, which is, BLS as well as LLB. However, the only difference between these law courses is that in a BLS LLB course aspirants are taught all the topics from a legal point of view right from the beginning which is not the case in a BA LLB or a course. law BA LLB BBA LLB In the first two years of a BLS LLB course, aspirants are taught subjects such as , , , and Legal Language and in the remaining three years of the course, candidates are taught subjects like , , , etc. Economics History Political Science English Contracts Constitution Family law Labour Laws As part of an integrated BLS LLB course, candidates have imparted education not only through classroom teaching methods but also by means of case studies and moot courts. BLS LLB Eligibility Criteria Candidates are eligible to pursue a BLS LLB course if they have completed their 10+2 from a recognised board. Apart from this, most popular law colleges also fix an additional (minimum) marks requirement that candidates need to fulfil in order to be eligible to apply for BLS LLB course offered by them. Thus, candidates belonging to General/ OBC/ Specially abled person categories need to secure at least 45% marks at their 10+2 level in order to pursue a BLS LLB course and the minimum percentage fixed for SC/ ST category is 40%. There are no age limit criteria that candidates need to fulfil in order to apply for BLS LLB course admissions. Some popular job profiles that candidates can pursue after securing a BLS LLB degree are as mentioned below: Job Profile Job Description Lawyer In this job profile, one needs to advise and represent clients in civil as well as criminal cases. Lawyers present cases in the court of law and take part in all proceedings and hearings. Solicitor In such a job profile an individual usually specialises in a specific area of law like tax, litigation, family or property. Solicitors offer legal advice to private as well as commercial clients. Advocate In such a job profile one needs to do a lot of research work to gather factual data as well as physical evidence to support their claim. Apart from this, other responsibilities allocated to advocates include scrutinising and drafting contracts. Legal Advisor Candidates opting to work in such a job profile are also lawyers who specialise in a specific field of law. Legal advisors are usually hired by governments as well as large organisations/ companies. The main task of a legal advisor is to protect their clients from any legal implication or consequence. Teacher or Lecturer After completing an LLB degree candidates can also teach law at the college or university level. An LLB course is can be pursued after completion of a bachelor degree, whereas the BLS LLB courses can be pursued right after Class 12th. Q. Where can I study BLS LLB course? A. There are many good colleges including, GNLU Gandhinagar and, the Government Law College, Mumbai offering admissions to BLS LLB course. Q. Is there any age limit criteria for BLS LLB course? A. No, there is no age limit criteria for admission to law courses, as per the regulations laid down by the Bar Council of India for legal education. Q.
