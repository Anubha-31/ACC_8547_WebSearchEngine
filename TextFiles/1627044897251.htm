Materials Science (also referred to as materials science and engineering) is an inter-disciplinary subject that involves the study of all materials starting from a glass or a piece of sport equipment to those used in medicine and aerospace. The subject explores the structure and design of any material and how it can be used in the most optimum way. Apart from this, the field of materials science also focuses on creation of new materials on the basis of different engineering requirements. The materials created through this field of science are used for a variety of purposes and range from nanotechnology to advanced polymers. Through their understanding of how materials work, materials scientists or engineers create new materials for new applications and develop existing materials to improve performance. Materials science is a broad field of study and contains plenty of opportunities for students who wish to build their career in it and its related technologies. UG Level th : Candidates should clear graduation preferably in materials science with at least 50% - 60% aggregate marks. PG Level : Candidates should clear post-graduation in materials science or a related discipline with minimum 60% - 75% marks. Candidates who clear (with minimum 90 – 95 percentile score) or and had Mathematics as a mandatory subject in graduation are also sometimes eligible to apply. PhD Level GATE CSIR UGC NET : Materials science eligibility criteria mentioned above might vary depending on the policy of the university in which a candidate is applying for admission. Materials Science: Job Profiles & Top Recruiters Candidates with a bachelor’s or master’s degree in materials science can find employment opportunities in a variety of industries such as automotive, manufacturing, pharmaceuticals, telecommunications, ceramic/ glass companies, nuclear, defence, oil & gas, etc. Some of the popular job profiles related to materials science are: : A material engineer specialises in metals, ceramics or plastics. S/he develops composite materials and studies the atomic structure of materials in order to discover new applications for their mechanical or chemical properties. Material Engineer : A material analyst, using different software systems designs material and supply movement plans for companies. Job responsibilities of a material analyst also includes tracking customer product demand, managing transportation of exports and imports, and developing productivity improvement proposals. Material Analyst : A materials scientist researches as well as studies the structures and chemical properties of different man-made and natural materials such as glass, metals, alloys, rubber, ceramics, etc. Materials Scientist : Job responsibilities of a materials technician include testing a material's strength and flexibility along with its characteristics such as chemical composition, cost assessment, safety and environmental impact, etc. Materials Technician : The job role of a manufacturing engineer includes developing manufacturing processes by studying requirements of the product and researching, designing, modifying as well as testing manufacturing methods and equipment. Manufacturing Engineer : A product tester is a professional who tests, evaluates and provides feedback of products that are being launched by a company. Product Tester : A metallurgist develops metal products and structures that range from small precision-made components to huge engineering parts. A metallurgist usually specialises in one area such as structural metallurgy, process or chemical.
